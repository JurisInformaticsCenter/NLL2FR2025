International Workshop on Translating Natural Legal Language into Formal Representation (NLL2FR 2025)

9 December 2025, University of Turin, Italy

Associated with JURIX 2025

Call for Contributions

While many rigorous frameworks for legal knowledge representation and reasoning have shown great potential, they often assume that legal knowledge can already be expressed in formal languages. In reality, however, most legal rules and case descriptions are written in natural language, creating a significant gap between natural legal language and formal representations.The aim of this workshop to bring together researchers and practitioners interested in bridging this gap between natural legal language and formal representations. Our interest extends beyond the translation of natural language rules into logical formulae to include the formalization of legal cases described in natural language.

Relevant topics include, but are not limited to:

  • Translating natural language rules into formal and logical representations
  • Constructing legal ontologies from natural language sources and documents
  • Extracting legal factors from case texts
  • Extracting and structuring argumentation from natural language sources
  • Addressing Technical and legal challenges in formalizing legal texts for computational applications
  • Presenting case studies and implementation reports on legal formalization systems
  • Developing reusable resources, including tools, logic patterns, and shared datasets
  • Verifying and validating formal legal representations in practice
  • Designing and applying Controlled Natural Languages (CNLs) as instruments for legal formalization
  • Applying Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques to legal rule extraction and formalization
  • Leveraging Large Language Models (LLMs) to translate legal texts into formal representations
